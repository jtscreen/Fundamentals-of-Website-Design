History

Discover the origins and evolution of Spiderman.

Spiderman, also known as Peter Parker, was created by writer Stan Lee and artist Steve Ditko and first appeared in Amazing Fantasy #15 in 1962, published by Marvel Comics.

Peter Parker was a high school student who gained spider-like abilities after being bitten by a radioactive spider during a science experiment. He decided to use his newfound powers to fight crime and protect the innocent, becoming the iconic superhero known as Spiderman.

Throughout the years, Spiderman has faced a variety of villains, including his arch-nemesis the Green Goblin, Doctor Octopus, Venom, and many others. He has also been a member of superhero teams such as the Avengers and the Fantastic Four.

Spiderman's character has undergone various iterations and adaptations in comic books, animated series, movies, and video games, making him one of the most recognizable and beloved superheroes worldwide.


Other Spider-Men

While Peter Parker is the most well-known Spider-Man, there have been several other individuals who have taken on the mantle and responsibilities of the iconic superhero:

  • Miles Morales: Created by writer Brian Michael Bendis and artist Sara Pichelli, Miles Morales first appeared in Ultimate Fallout #4 in 2011. He is a teenager who gains similar powers to Peter Parker after being bitten by a genetically-enhanced spider. Miles becomes Spider-Man in an alternate universe known as the Ultimate Universe and later joins the main Marvel Comics universe.
  • Ben Reilly: Introduced in The Amazing Spider-Man #149 in 1975, Ben Reilly is a clone of Peter Parker created by the villainous Jackal. Initially known as the Scarlet Spider, Ben takes on the identity of Spider-Man when Peter briefly retires. He later adopts the identity of the Scarlet Spider once again.
  • Miguel O'Hara: In the year 2099, geneticist Miguel O'Hara gains spider-like abilities through a genetic experiment. He becomes the Spider-Man of the future, protecting the city of Nueva York in a futuristic setting. Miguel O'Hara first appeared in Spider-Man 2099 #1 in 1992.
  • Gwen Stacy (Spider-Gwen/Ghost-Spider): In an alternate universe where Gwen Stacy is bitten by the radioactive spider instead of Peter Parker, she becomes the superhero known as Spider-Woman or Spider-Gwen. She first appeared in Edge of Spider-Verse #2 in 2014 and gained significant popularity, leading to her own comic book series.


These are just a few examples of the many individuals who have taken on the mantle of Spider-Man in various comic book storylines and alternate universes.

Iconic Spiderman Comics

The Amazing Spider-Man #1


The first issue of "The Amazing Spider-Man" series, featuring the debut of Spider-Man's iconic red and blue costume.



Spider-Verse

Spider-Verse completely changed the direction of Spider-Man comics forever. Now tied to a larger Web of Life and Destiny, Spider-Man would forever be associated with his variants from different universes. The "Spider-Verse" event would even extend far beyond the pages of Marvel Comics, resulting in the acclaimed Spider-Verse animated film franchise.

Spider-Man: The Clone Saga


This controversial storyline involves a clone of Peter Parker and had a significant impact on Spider-Man's continuity.



One More Day

As hated as "One More Day" is, it's essential to understanding Spider-Man's timeline in the Marvel Universe. The storyline features a massive reset for Spider-Man stories, erasing Peter Parker and Mary Jane Watson's marriage. "One More Day" leads into the "Brand New Day" initiative, which continued to reinvent Spider-Man's ongoing stories for many more years to come.

Kraven's Last Hunt

"Kraven's Last Hunt" is essential reading for any Spider-Man fan. Not only does the storyline mark Kraven the Hunter's best appearance in the history of Marvel Comics, but it's widely considered one of Spider-Man's best stories of all time. Far darker than most other Spider-Man comics, "Kraven's Last Hunt" inspired future Spider-Man arcs, including "The Child Within," "The Gauntlet," and "Grim Hunt."

Civil War

These "Back in Black" storylines were pivotal in Spider-Man's comic book history. In a heavily controversial move, Spider-Man revealed his secret identity to the world during the Civil War storyline, leading to increased threats to his personal life by several supervillains. When the Kingpin had Aunt May shot, however, Spider-Man beat him within an inch of his life in the ever-memorable finale of "Back in Black."

Superior Spider-Man

Not only did The Superior Spider-Man mark an unprecedented age in Spider-Man's life — since he was completely taken over by one of his greatest enemies — but the storyline also included several major changes to the brand. Most notably, the event included the creation of Parker Industries, which would become a focal point for the remainder of Slott's run on The Amazing Spider-Man.

Spider-Island

"Spider-Island" is often forgotten by Spider-Man fans despite its great importance to the character's ongoing stories. Besides being a fun and unique story, "Spider-Island" marks a turning point for Spider-Man as a superhero. After New York gets the chance to walk a mile in his shoes, Spider-Man finds himself much more popular in the public eye, shedding the "menace" label that had plagued him for years.

Maximus Carnage

The "Maximum Carnage" event takes place in issues of Spider-Man's ongoing comic books in the summer of 1993. In this massive storyline helmed by various different creative teams, Carnage unleashes havoc on New York City with his new "family" of supervillains, forcing Spider-Man to make a difficult choice about how to deal with this massive new threat.

Spider-Geddon

Spider-Geddon is the 2018 sequel to the original "Spider-Verse" event. It is one of the biggest multiverse events in Marvel Comics and becomes supremely important in understanding the state of the Spider-Verse. Despite not featuring Peter Parker's Spider-Man in a major role, Spider-Geddon gives Miles Morales his due, allowing him to take leadership over the Spider-Army

Other Media

Check out Spider-man's appearances in movies, TV shows, and more.

Movies

  • Spider-Man Trilogy (2002-2007) - Directed by Sam Raimi and starring Tobey Maguire as Spider-Man.
  • The Amazing Spider-Man Series (2012-2014) - Starring Andrew Garfield as Spider-Man.
  • Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse (2018) - An animated film featuring multiple Spider-People from different dimensions.

TV Shows

  • Spider-Man: The Animated Series (1994-1998) - A popular animated series from the 1990s.
  • Ultimate Spider-Man (2012-2017) - An animated series featuring a younger version of Spider-Man.

Video Games

  • Spider-Man (PS4, PS5, 2018) - A critically acclaimed action-adventure game developed by Insomniac Games.
  • Spider-Man 2: The Video Game (2004) - Based on the movie of the same name, this game is often considered one of the best Spider-Man games.

  • Spider-Man: Blue (2002-2003) - A miniseries exploring Spider-Man's early days and his relationship with Gwen Stacy.

Other Media
